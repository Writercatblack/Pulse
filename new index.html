PULSE

OCADU’s Creative Writing Program’s annual journal Pulse is officially open for submissions on Submittable (link below)!

We are currently accepting original, unpublished language/text-based submissions for:

  • Print (Including but not limited to: Poetry, prose, screenplay, excerpts, lyrics, hybrid/experimental writings, personal essays, opinion pieces, book/movie/art reviews, multilingual works, interdisciplinary works, typography, comics, graphic novels, and collaborative works within the fiction and/or nonfiction genres.)
  • Digital (All as listed above, and: video & audio works, spoken word, slam, music and original lyrics, video essays, animation, podcasts.)

Personal projects and school assignments are encouraged and welcome. If you are unsure whether a work would be right for Pulse, submit it anyways! We’d love to hear from you!

Here are our guidelines:

  • Maximum of 5 original and unpublished works per submission
  • Poetry: 1-5 poems
  • Prose: Maximum 4,000 words
  • Other written works (not limited to but including: interviews, book reviews, movie reviews, opinion pieces, personal essays, etc): Maximum 4,000 words.
  • Audio/Video Submissions (not limited to but including: music, original songs, podcasts, spoken word, slam performances, collaborative works, etc.): No more than 5 minutes.

Excerpts of longer works can be accepted as long as they can stand alone. If interested in submitting a longer piece, capped at 6,000 words, please email us ocadupulse@gmail.com. All work submitted must be previously unpublished. Simultaneous submissions are okay, but please let us know as soon as your work has been accepted elsewhere so we may wish you congratulations!

Please include a short (2-3 sentence) author's biography (written in third person) in your submission, with your name and/or pen name, preferred pronouns, the genre of your work(s), and all appropriate trigger warnings. Please note that we will not accept any works that includes any graphic violence/erotica, unexamined discriminatory/hateful language or actions, or any work that intends to cause harm to any other person or group of people. We are also not accepting fanworks, anything created by generative AI, or any audio/video submissions that include copyrighted music. Exercise your best judgment as to what work reflects both you as a writer as well as the community you write within.

We ask for first-time North American rights for accepted pieces. Following publication, all rights revert back to the author. Please note that your work first appeared in Pulse if it is reprinted elsewhere on the web or in print.

As an extension of the OCAD-U student body, Pulse 2024 acts in accordance with the professional codes of conduct while fulfilling the educational standards put forth by the University Board. We, as an integral part of representing a part of the larger student body at OCAD-U, align ourselves with OCAD-U's core values and ethics, and make the effort to accurately reflect such in our work.
